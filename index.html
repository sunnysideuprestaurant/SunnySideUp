Research

United States has fallen to the 6th place in Global Innovation Index
There is indeed an acute shortage of US workers with STEM skills – as reflected by an authoritative research published by the Brookings Institute

At the same time 73% of the teachers surveyed by National Association of Gifted Children (NAGC) conceded: “Too often, the brightest students are bored and under-challenged in school – we’re not giving them a sufficient chance to thrive”

A Gifted Child has advanced capacity to learn and apply what is learned, as compared to his or her peers (as per the definition from NAGC)

This advanced capacity requires modifications to the regular curriculum to ensure these children are challenged

Many gifted students may be so far ahead of their same-age peers that they know more than half of the grade-level curriculum before the school year begins

Gifted students may become bored or frustrated in an unchallenging classroom situation causing them to lose interest, learn bad study habits, or distrust the school environment
